Normal

This game mode is the normal version of MineSweeper. Diagonal mines are considered in all modes. Repeat guesses can be made but this will harm your performance. Play for time.

No successful attempts for mode 1

Haymaker

This game mode has a mine in either each row or each column (selected at random).

No successful attempts for mode 2

The Lightning Field

In this game mode, mines are added to the board as time progresses. You may recieve extra time if a duplicate mine is placed down

No successful attempts for mode 3

Pirate's Nightmare

In this game mode, the mine closest to your chosen cell will shift randomly. There is also a random chance that all mines will shift. Directions: Up/Down/Left/Right. Multiple mines may occupy a single node. Mines occupying visible cells will temporarily cover the number until they move.

No successful attempts for mode 4

Nuclear Decay

A random-sized cluster of mines will be randomly placed on the board. As time progresses, mines will separate from the cluster to random locations. Once they have separated, there is a chance that they will decay into two mines. This can only happen once per mine. Duplicates can also decay.

No successful attempts for mode 5

Symbiosis

As you play, the mines flocculate. The flocculate jumps around the board randomly while attracting individual mines, and varies in shape. When all mines have been collected, the flocculate redisperses as a random number of mines. Upon redispersion, each mine also wipes a visible cell.

No successful attempts for mode 6

Hell's Beacon

You have 2 minutes to determine where the mines are with only 5 guesses visible at a time. After 2 minutes, the board will invert and now cells with mines are empty and empty cells have mines. You must specify the now empty cells to return to the original board where the mines are in a new arrangement. The behaviour of the board is the same as the normal mode, however, visible cells will slowly disappear, with ones closest to the mines being hidden first.
